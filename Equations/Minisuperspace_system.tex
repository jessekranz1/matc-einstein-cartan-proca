\section{Reduced Minisuperspace Evolution System}

We evolve the reduced Bianchi-IX state vector:

\begin{equation}
\mathbf U =
(\Omega,\beta_+,\beta_-,A,\dot A,\phi,\dot\phi)
\end{equation}

using fourth-order Runge--Kutta integration.

The anisotropy equations are:

\begin{equation}
\ddot\beta_\pm + 3H\dot\beta_\pm + \frac{\partial V_{IX}}{\partial \beta_\pm} = \Pi_\pm^{(A)}
\end{equation}

where:

\begin{equation}
\Pi_\pm^{(A)}
\end{equation}

is the axial-vector anisotropic stress.

The axial-vector evolution equation is:

\begin{equation}
\ddot A + 3H\dot A + M_A^2 A
=
S_A
\end{equation}

with source:

\begin{equation}
S_A
\propto
-\frac{2\alpha}{M_*}
\tilde F^{\mu 0}\dot\phi
\end{equation}

The scalar equation is:

\begin{equation}
\ddot\phi + 3H\dot\phi + V'(\phi)
=
-\frac{\alpha}{M_*}F\tilde F
\end{equation}

The Hamiltonian residual is monitored using:

\begin{equation}
\mathcal R_H
=
\left|
1 - \frac{\rho_{total}}{3H^2}
\right|
\end{equation}

A successful numerical evolution satisfies:

\begin{equation}
\mathcal R_H < 10^{-6}
\end{equation}

throughout the nonsingular transition.
