\section{Constraint Structure and Degrees of Freedom}

The reduced Einstein--Cartan--Proca EFT possesses the canonical momenta:

\begin{equation}
\pi^i = \frac{\partial \mathcal L}{\partial \dot A_i}
\end{equation}

while:

\begin{equation}
\pi^0 \approx 0
\end{equation}

appears as a primary constraint.

Demanding preservation of the primary constraint yields the secondary constraint:

\begin{equation}
\partial_i \pi^i + M_A^2 A_0 \approx 0
\end{equation}

This algebraically fixes the temporal component \(A_0\).

Therefore \(A_0\) is non-propagating and acts as a Lagrange multiplier.

The physical propagating degrees of freedom are:

\begin{itemize}
\item 2 tensor graviton modes
\item 3 massive axial-vector modes
\item 1 scalar mode
\end{itemize}

for a total of:

\begin{equation}
N_{DoF} = 6
\end{equation}

The reduced model is therefore free of the longitudinal ghost instability that commonly affects unconstrained vector-tensor theories.
